**Section 8** is the common name for the **Housing Choice Voucher (HCV)** program run by the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). It helps very low-income families, elderly people, and people with disabilities afford decent housing in the private market.

## How it works

1. You apply through a local **Public Housing Authority (PHA)**.
2. If selected, the PHA issues a **voucher**.
3. You find a rental that passes inspection and meets the PHA's payment standard.
4. The voucher pays part of the rent directly to the landlord; you pay the rest (your **tenant share**).

Your share is typically around **30% of your adjusted monthly income**.

## Who qualifies?

Eligibility is based on:

- **Income** — generally must be at or below 50% of your area's median income (HUD "very low income" limit).
- **Household size** — limits vary by how many people live with you.
- **Citizenship or eligible immigration status**.
- **Local preferences** — many PHAs prioritize veterans, elderly, disabled, or working families.

## How much does Section 8 pay?

The voucher covers the difference between your **Total Tenant Payment (TTP)** and the **payment standard** set by your PHA (usually based on HUD Fair Market Rents). If you rent a unit that costs more than the payment standard, you pay the extra.

## How to apply

There is no single national application. Each PHA has its own waiting list — and many open only for short windows every few years. Start by:

1. **Finding your PHA** on [HUD's directory](https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/public_indian_housing/pha/contacts).
2. **Checking if the waiting list is open**.
3. **Submitting an application** when it opens.
